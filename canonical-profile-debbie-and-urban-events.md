This document is the clean working profile to use for future support, strategy, drafting, and operational assistance. It is based on retained memory and context, not a verbatim interview transcript.

## 1. Core business context

Urban Events Ltd is the New Zealand parent company behind a portfolio of premium polo event brands:
- Lexus Urban Polo in New Zealand
- F3Polo in Australia
- Portsea Polo in Victoria, Australia

The business generates revenue through four main lines:
- sponsorship
- private marquees
- VIP ticketing
- general admission ticketing

The business is positioned as premium, aspirational, and high-status. Customers are buying more than a ticket. They are buying hospitality, networking, client hosting, social visibility, entertainment, and access to a high-end experience.

## 2. Strategic truth to keep front of mind

The commercial engine of the business is sponsorship.

Sponsorship is the best-margin revenue line and the main underwriting mechanism for events. The most important current commercial priority is securing a major sponsor in Australia. Without sponsorship, event risk rises sharply.

Private marquees are the highest-value hospitality product and should be treated as a major commercial priority, not a side product.

Premium brand value must be protected. Discounting should not be treated as a default growth tactic.

## 3. Simon Wilson profile

Simon is the Managing Director and the main commercial driver of the business.

He leads:
- sales
- sponsor conversations
- the marketing look and feel
- high-level commercial direction

He is strongest at:
- selling the vision
- developing marketing ideas
- getting sponsors to believe in the opportunity

He is most focused on:
- revenue
- sponsorship
- venues

He is energised by:
- conversations with brands and businesses that want to be involved

He is drained by:
- admin
- staff issues
- government delays around venue and field bookings

His decision style:
- uses both instinct and numbers
- trusts ticket sales, cash flow, and returning customers most
- moves fast on getting sales online
- moves slower on hiring and spending
- is selectively risk tolerant and will wait when uncertain

What he wants from the assistant:
- blunt advice
- genuinely strong ideas only
- help finding sponsorship leads
- help finding marquee buyers
- help with outreach that converts
- help understanding margins and conversion more clearly
- help reducing friction so deals close faster

## 5. Brand and market positioning

The business should be presented as:
- a premium lifestyle event
- a premium sporting and entertainment spectacle
- exceptional hospitality
- prestige
- memorable client experiences
- an experience like no other

Debbie believes the market often underestimates:
- the commercial value of private marquee hosting
- the premium nature of the event
- the networking value across marquees
- how enjoyable the event is
- the sporting quality of the players and ponies

## 6. What is working

Current strengths include:
- strong premium positioning
- repeat attendance
- sponsor demand
- event experience
- partnerships
- pricing confidence
- delivery capability
- sales process and team execution
- email marketing where a real database exists

## 7. What is fragile

Current weak points and constraints include:
- sponsorship sourcing depends heavily on direct sales
- missed leads and missed follow-up can cost revenue
- campaign execution takes time and money
- venue and government deadlines can slip
- cash, time, leads, and databases are all constraints
- too much prior work gets repeated instead of systemised

## 8. Assistant operating rules for this account

When supporting Debbie, Simon, or Urban Events:
- protect premium positioning
- do not casually suggest discounting
- be especially careful with customer-facing and prospect-facing wording
- optimise for revenue, sponsorship, and conversion
- recognise that sponsorship and marquee sales cycles are longer than they look
- reduce repetitive operational work wherever possible
- prefer practical systems, templates, and process clarity over abstract advice
- be blunt when necessary, but not careless
